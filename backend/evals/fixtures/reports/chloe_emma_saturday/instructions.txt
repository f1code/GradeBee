MOTIVATION AND PARTICIPATION:
o Motivation: definition: factor or group of factors that bring about a specific type of behaviour. This section should comment on whether the student shows a positive attitude towards different activities conducted in the classroom:
▪ How does the student come into the classroom?
▪ Do they show willingness to learn?
▪ Are they open to doing new activities?
▪ Are they cooperative with the rest of the group and with the teacher?
o Participation: definition: involvement in an event, act or activity. Parents must be informed whether their child participates in the activities that are carried out in the classroom and whether or not they put previously-acquired knowledge, as well as language currently being worked on, into practice.
▪ Outline the degree of participation based on what the student views as being important. For example, he/she is very competitive and always wants to win. He/she gets cross when they don't win. He/she always wants to come first.
▪ Another noteworthy aspect is whether the student holds back when new material is introduced, and whether they take part to a lesser degree than when internalised knowledge is elicited in class.
BEHAVIOUR: definition: the way in which a person behaves in a specific or general situation.
o Note whether the student is integrated within the group or whether they are easily distracted or unable to focus.
o Point out, if necessary, how a student is reacting in light of situations that have had to be tackled with a specific plan.
o Explain whether the environment or general mood of the group, or the student's particular mood, affects the student's behaviour. Is the group more easily distracted than normal? Is the student especially tired on rainy days, after public holidays, etc.?
