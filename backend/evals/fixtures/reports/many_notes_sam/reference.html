Term report (3rd term)

Data

  • Name: Thomas
  • Teacher: Clarisse Bydens

Participation/Motivation

Assessment (Participation / Motivation): Good

Comment:
Thomas usually came to class in a good mood and showed interest in the activities. He particularly seemed to enjoy the "Let's go to the zoo" activity, the "Why are you so sad?" story and the routines. However, he was sometimes a bit uncooperative and needed reminders to stay involved and participate with the group. At times, he had to be encouraged to join in with the class, but when focused, he was able to follow along well.

Learning process

Assessment (Learning process): Good

Comment:
Thomas's English knowledge is good. He can understand and repeat the vocabulary and structures worked on in class. He was able to tell the colours, the genders and the moods, and he could also repeat the weather and do the gestures for it. He could count very well and matched the dot cards with the correct numbers successfully. He sometimes needed a little help to remember the full structure, but he showed a good understanding of the content and could respond well when supported.

Behaviour

Assessment (Behaviour): Acceptable

Comment:
Thomas is a quiet student who can be attentive and cooperative when he is engaged in the lesson. He sometimes needed to be accompanied more closely into the activities and was not always willing to participate with the group. On one occasion, he did not respect the classroom materials and needed several reminders to handle them properly. Nevertheless, he was able to show his understanding during the activities and, when focused, he worked well with the teacher and the class.

Marks

Oral Test Mark: 9.00 Very good. Thomas could do the test very well and showed a good understanding of the vocabulary and structures.

Final comment

Throughout the term, Thomas has shown that he can understand and use the language well, especially when he is focused and willing to take part. He has made good progress in his oral comprehension and in his ability to repeat the structures learnt in class. It would be beneficial to continue listening to the audio track at home in order to keep in mind the vocabulary words and structures learnt throughout the school year. The teacher would like to encourage Thomas to keep improving his participation and consistency in class.
