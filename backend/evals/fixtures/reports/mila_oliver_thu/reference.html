Term report (3rd term)

Data

  • Name: Mila
  • Teacher: Thérèse Garnier
  • Level: Oliver Thursday 2025/2026

Participation/Motivation

Assessment (Participation / Motivation): Good

Comment: Mila generally comes to class in a positive mood and shows interest in the activities presented. She is usually willing to participate and can be very engaged when she feels comfortable with the task, especially in action-based activities and games. At times, however, she can become upset when things do not go as she expects, which affects her willingness to continue taking part. With encouragement, she is able to rejoin the class and show good motivation, particularly when she has the opportunity to help or demonstrate what she knows.

Learning process

Assessment (Learning process): Good

Comment: Mila has shown good understanding of the structures and vocabulary worked on this term. She can repeat the target language well and, in many activities, uses the new language with confidence. Her knowledge of the story has improved notably, and she is able to retell parts of it with little support. She also performs very well in activities involving weather, clothing, and colour vocabulary. At times, she still needs help with some specific words and routines, such as taking off and putting on, and she may need further practice to remain consistent when new language is introduced. Continued repetition and reinforcement will help her consolidate her progress even further.

Behaviour

Assessment (Behaviour): Good

Comment: Mila is well integrated in the group and can participate positively in class. On the whole, she behaves appropriately and works well with the rest of the students, especially during practical and familiar activities. However, when she becomes frustrated or does not get her way, she may withdraw, refuse to participate, or show signs of anger, which can affect her focus during the lesson. She sometimes needs reminders to stay engaged and remain seated during story time. When she is calm, she responds well and shows a much more cooperative attitude.

Marks

Oral Test Mark: 8.00    Good. Mila can understand the question and answers either with a single word or with a structure which is only partially correct.

Final comment

Mila has made good progress this term and has shown that she can understand and use a great deal of the language worked on in class. Her ability to repeat structures, recognise vocabulary, and take part in action activities is very promising. With continued encouragement to manage moments of frustration and to stay engaged throughout the whole lesson, she will be able to keep building confidence and continue developing her English skills successfully.
